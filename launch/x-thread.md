# X / Twitter launch thread

**1/**
Every AI writes the same way. delve, tapestry, "not just X, it's Y," em dashes everywhere, "in conclusion."

You can feel it. So can your readers.

I built a free tool that makes any AI stop. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Kimi.

🧵

**2/**
It's based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" page. Their editors fight machine-generated articles all day, so they wrote down every tell.

I turned that list into 12 hard rules a model can actually follow.

**3/**
What it kills:
- the banned words (delve, underscore, vibrant, leverage)
- fake significance ("stands as a testament")
- "not just a tool, a movement"
- em dash overuse
- "experts say" with no source
- the "In conclusion" wrap-up

**4/**
It works two ways.

Claude: install it as a skill, it triggers on "humanize this."

ChatGPT / Gemini / Grok / Kimi: paste one prompt into custom instructions. Done.

**5/**
For coders: drop one AGENTS.md in your repo and Cursor, Antigravity, Codex, and Claude Code all read it.

There's also a CLI that scans your draft and prints every tell with a fix.

**6/**
Laziest install: paste the repo link into your AI and say "read INSTALL-FOR-AI.md and set yourself up."

It detects what it is and installs itself.
