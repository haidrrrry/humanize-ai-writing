# Rewrite rules — fix each tell

For every tell in `ai-tells.md`, the fix and a before/after. Preserve meaning and
facts. When a claim has no source, cut it — don't fabricate a replacement.

## Banned vocabulary → plain words
- **Before:** "This delve into the intricate tapestry underscores its pivotal role."
- **After:** "This shows why it matters."
- Swap: delve→look at, intricate→complex/detailed, underscore→show, pivotal→key,
  leverage→use, foster→build/encourage, robust→strong/reliable, utilize→use.

## Fake significance → state what it is
- **Before:** "The library stands as a testament to the city's enduring
  commitment to learning."
- **After:** "The library opened in 1931 and holds 200,000 volumes."
- Cut "stands as," "serves as a testament," "pivotal moment," "indelible mark."

## Present-participle padding → cut or convert to a fact
- **Before:** "The station opened in 1890, contributing to the region's growth."
- **After:** "The station opened in 1890. Freight traffic tripled within a
  decade." (only if true; otherwise just the first sentence.)

## Negative parallelism → direct positive claim
- **Before:** "It's not just a phone, it's a lifestyle."
- **After:** "The phone syncs your calendar, health data, and home devices."
- **Before:** "Not only fast but also reliable."
- **After:** "Fast and reliable." (or pick the one that matters.)

## Rule of three → use the real count
- **Before:** "Affordable, scalable, and future-proof."
- **After:** "Cheaper than the alternatives, and it scales." (two real points.)

## Punctuation
- Em dashes: keep at most one per few paragraphs; otherwise use a period or comma.
- Replace curly quotes "" '' with straight " '.
- Remove bold used for emphasis. Headings → sentence case.

## Promotional tone → peer voice
- **Before:** "Our cutting-edge platform is committed to delivering world-class,
  seamless experiences."
- **After:** "The platform loads in under a second and works offline."

## Weasel attribution → name it or cut it
- **Before:** "Experts say the method is widely used."
- **After:** "A 2023 Stanford survey found 60% of teams use it." (if sourced.)
- If no source exists: delete the sentence.

## Hollow conclusion → end on the last real point
- **Before:** "In conclusion, this tool offers many benefits and has a bright
  future ahead."
- **After:** (delete it; stop at the final concrete sentence.)

## Rhythm → vary it
- Break up uniform medium-length sentences. Add a short one. Allow a fragment.
- Let a key term repeat instead of cycling synonyms.

## What NOT to do while humanizing
- Don't add slang or fake casualness to "sound human" — that's its own tell.
- Don't change the facts, numbers, names, or the author's position.
- Don't pad to hit a length. Shorter is usually more human.
- Don't replace one banned word with another banned word.
